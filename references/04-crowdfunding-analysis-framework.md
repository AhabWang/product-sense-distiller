# Crowdfunding Analysis Framework

This file defines the crowdfunding analysis framework for Product Sense Distiller.

Use this file when analyzing Kickstarter, Indiegogo, pre-order campaigns, crowdfunding landing pages, early adopter launches, or any product that uses story-driven early sales to validate demand.

The purpose is not to summarize a campaign page.

The purpose is to judge:

- Why users backed the product
- What value anchor triggered payment
- Whether the campaign success reflects real demand or novelty-driven curiosity
- Whether the product can become a long-term business after crowdfunding

---

## 1. Core Belief

Crowdfunding is not the same as product-market fit.

Crowdfunding can validate:

- Early adopter interest
- Story attractiveness
- Product hook clarity
- Price sensitivity under discount
- Category novelty
- Community resonance
- Visual demo effectiveness
- Founder credibility
- Pre-order conversion

But crowdfunding does not fully validate:

- Retail price acceptance
- Long-term retention
- Mass-market demand
- Manufacturing reliability
- Review quality
- After-sales cost
- Channel scalability
- Repeat purchase
- Brand durability
- Competitive defense

Useful judgment:

> 众筹能证明“有人愿意为这个故事下注”，但不能直接证明“这个生意能长期跑”。

---

## 2. Campaign Summary

Start by extracting the basic facts.

Analyze:

- Product name
- Platform
- Campaign status
- Funding amount
- Backer count
- Average order value
- Main reward tiers
- Super early bird price
- Early bird price
- Standard crowdfunding price
- Expected retail price
- Shipping timeline
- Creator background
- Previous campaign history
- Category
- Core claim

If data is unavailable, say it clearly.

Ask:

- How much did it raise?
- How many people backed it?
- What is the approximate AOV?
- Is the funding driven by many users or a small number of high-price orders?
- Is the project a breakout success or only moderate validation?
- Is the funding number inflated by high-ticket bundles?

Useful judgment:

> 众筹金额要拆开看：金额、人数、客单价、价格结构，缺一不可。

---

## 3. Category and Timing

Judge the category before judging the campaign.

Ask:

- Is this a new category, growing category, crowded category, mature category, or hype category?
- Are users already educated?
- Are there dominant competitors?
- Are alternatives already good enough?
- Is the product riding a trend?
- Is the campaign creating demand or harvesting existing demand?
- Is the category suitable for crowdfunding?
- Is the timing early, right, or late?

Category timing types:

### Greenfield Category

Users are not fully educated. Storytelling and education matter.

### Growing Category

Demand is rising. Differentiation and trust matter.

### Crowded Category

Users have many alternatives. Value anchor and price logic matter.

### Mature Category

Incremental improvement is not enough unless it solves a sharp pain.

### Hype Category

Attention is high, but demand quality may be weak.

Useful judgment:

> 品类阶段决定打法。新兴品类卖想象，拥挤品类卖硬差异，成熟品类卖确定性。

---

## 4. Product Hook

The product hook is why users stop scrolling.

Analyze:

- What is the first thing users notice?
- Is the hook visual, technical, emotional, functional, price-based, or identity-based?
- Can the hook be explained in one sentence?
- Does the hook create curiosity or buying intent?
- Is the hook strong enough for social sharing?
- Is the hook useful in real life, or only attractive on the page?

Hook types:

### Visual Hook

The product looks different, beautiful, futuristic, cute, or surprising.

### Technical Hook

The product uses a new technology, sensor, mechanism, algorithm, or material.

### Scenario Hook

The product solves a recognizable use case.

### Pain-removal Hook

The product removes a hated task.

### Metric Hook

The product measures something users could not measure before.

### Emotional Hook

The product provides comfort, reassurance, companionship, safety, or identity.

### Price Hook

The product looks like a strong deal versus retail or competitors.

Useful judgment:

> 好 hook 负责让用户停下来，但真正的价值锚点负责让用户掏钱。

---

## 5. Target Backer Segment

Crowdfunding backers are not the same as mass-market users.

Ask:

- Who is most likely to back this campaign?
- Are they early adopters, hobbyists, professionals, parents, patients, creators, geeks, outdoor users, cyclists, pet owners, gamers, or design lovers?
- Are they buying utility, novelty, identity, status, control, anxiety relief, or community belonging?
- Is the target segment small but high-pain?
- Can the product expand beyond early adopters?
- Is the buyer the same as the user?
- Who influences the purchase decision?

Typical crowdfunding users:

- Tech enthusiasts
- Prosumer users
- Hobbyists
- Niche high-pain users
- Early adopters
- Design-sensitive consumers
- Productivity seekers
- Health-anxious users
- Outdoor/adventure users
- Parents seeking better solutions
- Community-driven users

Useful judgment:

> 众筹用户经常比大众用户更宽容、更好奇，也更愿意为新奇感付钱。

---

## 6. Problem Definition

Do not accept the campaign's stated problem at face value.

Ask:

- What exact problem does the campaign claim to solve?
- Is this problem real?
- Who experiences it most painfully?
- How often does it happen?
- What do users do today?
- Why are existing alternatives insufficient?
- Is the problem severe enough to justify a new device?
- Is the problem urgent, repeated, expensive, emotionally heavy, or identity-related?
- Is the problem exaggerated for storytelling?

Classify the problem:

- Real high-pain problem
- Repeated efficiency problem
- Trust or safety problem
- Health anxiety problem
- Emotional comfort problem
- Hobbyist/prosumer upgrade problem
- Nice-to-have convenience problem
- Story-inflated problem
- Pseudo-demand

Useful judgment:

> 众筹页面最会把小麻烦讲成大痛点，所以要把问题强度重新校准。

---

## 7. Value Anchor

Every crowdfunding analysis must identify the value anchor.

Ask:

- What is the strongest reason to back?
- Is it clear in the first 10 seconds?
- Is it shown through a strong demo?
- Is it functional, emotional, social, health-related, efficiency-driven, safety-driven, or identity-driven?
- Does the value anchor justify the early-bird price?
- Does it justify the expected retail price?
- Is the value anchor stronger than the product's novelty?
- Would users still buy if the product looked less new?

Common crowdfunding value anchors:

- Save time
- Remove effort
- Improve safety
- Create new capability
- Measure new data
- Automate a painful task
- Provide emotional comfort
- Upgrade a hobby workflow
- Replace multiple tools
- Offer premium design at lower price
- Create identity belonging

Useful judgment:

> 如果用户下单主要是因为“新奇”，那它是众筹机会；如果用户下单是因为“我真的需要”，才有长期生意的可能。

---

## 8. Innovation Type

Classify the campaign's innovation.

### Replacement Innovation

It replaces an existing solution by being cheaper, faster, easier, safer, more portable, or more convenient.

### Incremental Innovation

It improves an existing category through design, integration, automation, precision, comfort, or intelligence.

### Category-creating Innovation

It creates a new metric, new behavior, new use case, or new category logic.

### Story Innovation

It packages existing technology into a more compelling story or identity.

### Form-factor Innovation

It changes the physical form, portability, wearability, or interaction style.

Ask:

- Is the innovation technically meaningful?
- Is it experientially meaningful?
- Is it commercially meaningful?
- Can users perceive it?
- Does it create a purchase reason?
- Does it create a moat?
- Can competitors copy it quickly?
- Is the innovation only strong in campaign copy?

Useful judgment:

> 众筹最常见的陷阱，是把“讲法创新”误判成“产品创新”。

---

## 9. Page Storytelling

A crowdfunding page is a sales system.

Analyze how the page builds conversion:

- Hero section
- Headline
- Subheadline
- Main visual
- GIF / video demo
- Problem setup
- Before-after comparison
- Feature explanation
- Use-case storytelling
- Social proof
- Technical proof
- Team credibility
- Manufacturing proof
- Timeline
- Rewards
- FAQ
- Risk disclosure
- Comment response

Ask:

- Does the page make the problem clear?
- Does it show the product working?
- Does it show real use scenarios?
- Does it translate features into benefits?
- Does it build trust before asking for money?
- Does it make the price feel like a deal?
- Does it reduce delivery anxiety?
- Does it answer obvious objections?

Useful judgment:

> 好众筹页面不是介绍产品，而是一步步把用户从“好奇”推到“愿意下注”。

---

## 10. Demo Quality

Crowdfunding users need proof.

Analyze the demo:

- Is there a real prototype?
- Is the demo live or rendered?
- Does the video show actual use?
- Does it show before and after?
- Does it show edge cases?
- Does it show real size, weight, noise, speed, and setup?
- Does it hide difficult parts?
- Does the demo prove the core claim?
- Does the demo make the value instantly understandable?
- Does the demo reduce trust cost?

Weak demo signals:

- Too many renderings
- No real user interaction
- No continuous shot
- No failure cases
- No manufacturing proof
- No real environment
- Heavy reliance on animation
- Overly perfect scenarios
- No third-party validation
- No explanation of limitations

Useful judgment:

> Demo 不只是展示功能，而是降低用户对“这东西到底能不能做出来”的怀疑。

---

## 11. Pricing and Reward Design

Pricing is one of the most important parts of crowdfunding analysis.

Analyze:

- Super early bird price
- Early bird price
- Standard crowdfunding price
- Expected retail price
- Bundle pricing
- Add-ons
- Shipping cost
- Deposit strategy
- Discount depth
- Price ladder
- Scarcity mechanism
- Upgrade tiers
- Family packs or multi-unit packs

Ask:

- Is the early-bird price attractive?
- Is the discount believable?
- Is the retail price inflated to make the discount look larger?
- What is the real price users are accepting?
- Does the price match category expectations?
- Does the value anchor justify the price?
- Does the campaign rely too much on discount urgency?
- Is the AOV high because of bundles?
- Would conversion survive at retail price?

Useful judgment:

> 众筹价验证的是“早鸟价下的购买意愿”，不是天然验证零售价成立。

---

## 12. Trust Building

Trust cost is central in crowdfunding.

Backers are paying before delivery, so they must trust:

- Product feasibility
- Creator capability
- Manufacturing readiness
- Delivery timeline
- Quality control
- Certification path
- Safety
- Customer support
- Refund policy
- Communication quality

Trust-building assets:

- Working prototype
- Founder video
- Previous successful campaigns
- Factory footage
- Certification progress
- Manufacturing partners
- Press coverage
- Influencer review
- Expert endorsement
- User testimonials
- Transparent timeline
- Detailed FAQ
- Risk disclosure
- Frequent updates

Trust red flags:

- Anonymous team
- No real prototype
- Unrealistic delivery timeline
- Overpromised features
- Vague manufacturing plan
- Weak FAQ
- No certification explanation
- No response to comments
- Excessive renderings
- No real reviews
- Too many stretch goals

Useful judgment:

> 众筹卖的不只是产品，还有“我相信你能把它交付出来”。

---

## 13. Comment and Feedback Signals

Comments are often more revealing than the campaign page.

Analyze comment signals:

### Purchase Motivation

- Why did users back?
- What feature excited them?
- What use case do they mention?

### Objections

- What are users worried about?
- Price?
- Shipping?
- Compatibility?
- Certification?
- Safety?
- Durability?
- App?
- Privacy?

### Trust Issues

- Are users asking for proof?
- Are users doubting timeline?
- Are users comparing previous failed campaigns?
- Are users asking for real footage?

### Feature Requests

- Are users asking for important missing features?
- Are requests aligned with the core value or distracting?

### Sentiment

- Excited
- Curious
- Skeptical
- Confused
- Frustrated
- Angry
- Waiting for proof

### Post-campaign Signals

- Delays
- Delivery complaints
- Quality complaints
- Refund requests
- Support issues
- Positive reviews
- Repeat purchase interest

Useful judgment:

> 页面告诉你团队想让用户相信什么，评论区告诉你用户真正担心什么。

---

## 14. Conversion Logic

Identify why users back now.

Possible conversion drivers:

- Early-bird discount
- Limited quantity
- Strong novelty
- Clear pain
- Community identity
- Fear of missing out
- Founder credibility
- Strong demo
- Category timing
- Social proof
- Press coverage
- Influencer trust
- High perceived value
- Emotional resonance
- Professional workflow need
- Health or safety anxiety

Ask:

- What makes the user act now instead of later?
- Is conversion driven by urgency or real need?
- Is the user buying utility or identity?
- Is the product easy to explain to friends?
- Does the campaign create a “this is made for me” moment?
- Does it solve a painful problem better than alternatives?
- Does the page remove enough uncertainty?

Useful judgment:

> 好的众筹转化不是只靠便宜，而是让用户觉得“这东西现在不买，我会错过”。

---

## 15. Competitive Context

Crowdfunding products often look unique until compared properly.

Analyze:

- Direct competitors
- Indirect alternatives
- Manual workaround
- App-based alternative
- Existing hardware alternative
- Professional solution
- DIY solution
- Cheaper substitute
- Premium substitute
- Brand-name competitor

Ask:

- What does this product do better?
- What does it do worse?
- Is the difference obvious to users?
- Is the difference worth paying for?
- Is the product entering a category with established expectations?
- Is the product educating a new category?
- Can existing players copy it?
- Does it have any defensibility beyond first-mover attention?

Useful judgment:

> 不要只和页面里选出来的竞品比，要和用户真实会用的替代方案比。

---

## 16. Hardware Feasibility in Crowdfunding

Crowdfunding hardware must be judged harshly.

Ask:

- Is the prototype real?
- Is the core technology proven?
- Are key components mature?
- Is the industrial design manufacturable?
- Are there moving parts?
- Are there water, heat, pressure, laser, battery, or sensor risks?
- Is the app or AI feature critical?
- Is certification required?
- Is tooling needed?
- Is the shipping timeline realistic?
- Is the production plan credible?
- Are supply chain partners disclosed?
- Are risks acknowledged?

Crowdfunding hardware red flags:

- Unrealistic delivery timeline
- Too many complex functions
- Heavy reliance on custom components
- No factory proof
- No certification path
- No reliability testing
- Big claims with weak evidence
- Small team tackling difficult hardware
- Low price that does not seem to cover BOM
- Excessive stretch goals that increase complexity

Useful judgment:

> 众筹硬件最怕“页面已经卖爆了，产品其实还没过工程现实”。

---

## 17. Long-term Business Risk

Every crowdfunding case must answer:

Is this a long-term business, or just a successful campaign?

Ask:

- Does the problem repeat?
- Does the product have retention?
- Does the category have scale?
- Can it survive retail price?
- Can it survive Amazon reviews?
- Can it survive copycats?
- Can the brand build trust?
- Can the company support after-sales?
- Can channel expansion work?
- Is there a product roadmap?
- Is there accessory, service, subscription, consumable, or upgrade potential?
- Can CAC decrease over time?
- Can word-of-mouth form?

Long-term risk types:

- Novelty fades
- Usage frequency too low
- Price only works in early bird
- Mass users less tolerant than backers
- Supply chain cannot scale
- Review quality damages conversion
- Competitors copy quickly
- Category too narrow
- No repeat purchase
- No channel fit
- High after-sales cost

Useful judgment:

> 众筹是起点，不是护城河。真正的考验是交付、评价、复购和渠道。

---

## 18. Common Crowdfunding Archetypes

Classify the campaign if useful.

### Strong Pain Solver

Solves a real, repeated, painful task.

Usually has stronger long-term potential.

### Cool Gadget

Highly novel and shareable, but long-term usage uncertain.

Good crowdfunding potential, fragile business potential.

### Prosumer Tool

Serves creators, engineers, designers, cyclists, outdoor users, or hobbyists.

Often has strong niche demand.

### Emotional Comfort Product

Sells companionship, relaxation, sleep, safety, or reassurance.

Requires strong trust and retention validation.

### New Metric Product

Measures something mainstream products do not measure.

Can create category opportunity if users understand the metric.

### Premium Repackaging

Makes an existing product more beautiful, integrated, or premium.

Needs strong brand and design execution.

### AI Hardware Wrapper

Packages AI capability into dedicated hardware.

Must prove new interaction or scenario, not just “ChatGPT in a box.”

### Halo Product

Not necessarily mass-market.

Builds brand imagination, PR, and ecosystem pull.

Useful judgment:

> 先判断它是哪类众筹产品，再判断它该用什么标准衡量。

---

## 19. Data Interpretation

When data is available, interpret carefully.

### Funding Amount

High funding means strong campaign performance, not automatically strong business.

### Backer Count

Backer count shows demand breadth better than funding amount alone.

### AOV

AOV helps judge whether funding is driven by many users or high-ticket bundles.

### Early-bird Sellout

May indicate strong urgency or limited discount attraction.

### Comment Volume

Shows engagement, but sentiment matters more than volume.

### Updates

Shows creator communication quality and execution seriousness.

### Delivery Timeline

A short timeline can signal readiness or unrealistic planning.

### Refund / Complaint Signals

Strong indicators of delivery and quality risk.

Useful judgment:

> 数据不要只看大数字，要看数字背后的用户行为和价格结构。

---

## 20. Campaign Analysis Output Template

When analyzing a crowdfunding campaign, use this structure when appropriate:

1. One-sentence verdict
2. Campaign summary
3. Category and timing
4. Product hook
5. Target backer segment
6. Problem definition
7. Value anchor
8. Innovation type
9. Page storytelling
10. Pricing and reward design
11. Trust-building elements
12. Comment and feedback signals
13. Hardware feasibility risks
14. Long-term business risk
15. Lessons for product managers
16. Final judgment

Do not force every section if the user asks for a short answer.

---

## 21. One-sentence Verdict Patterns

Use direct verdicts.

Examples:

- 这是一个很适合众筹的产品，但长期生意还没有被证明。
- 它的 hook 很强，价值锚点也清楚，但量产和售后风险不能低估。
- 它筹得好，不是因为品类大，而是因为切中了一个小众高痛人群。
- 它看起来像新品类，实际上是把已有需求用更性感的方式重新包装了一遍。
- 它的问题不是没有创新，而是创新点还没有强到支撑零售价。
- 它的众筹成功更多来自新奇感和早鸟价格，不一定代表大众市场成立。
- 它真正聪明的地方，是用一个新指标避开了主流竞品的正面战场。
- 它不是走量产品，更像一个拉高品牌想象力的 halo product。
- 这个项目最大的启发不是功能，而是它如何把技术故事翻译成购买理由。

---

## 22. Lessons Extraction

Every crowdfunding analysis should end with transferable lessons.

Extract lessons for:

- Product definition
- User segmentation
- Value proposition
- Page storytelling
- Pricing strategy
- Trust building
- Hardware validation
- Comment mining
- GTM
- Long-term business design

Good lesson examples:

- 不要只卖功能，要把功能翻译成用户在具体场景里的购买理由。
- 众筹页面第一屏必须讲清楚 hook，但真正让用户付款的是价值锚点。
- 早鸟价可以制造转化，但不能替代零售价的价值证明。
- 小众高痛人群比泛泛大众人群更适合早期众筹。
- 如果硬件复杂度高，页面必须提前建立制造和交付信任。
- 众筹项目最怕讲了一个很大的未来，但没有证明第一个版本能稳定交付。

Useful judgment:

> 案例分析的价值，不是知道别人怎么成功，而是知道我们下次怎么少犯错。

---

## 23. Final Crowdfunding Judgment

End with one of these judgments when appropriate:

- Strong crowdfunding product, uncertain long-term business
- Strong niche demand, mass-market expansion uncertain
- Good story, weak product proof
- Good product hook, weak price justification
- Real pain point, difficult hardware execution
- Strong early adopter appeal, retention unproven
- Strong technical story, weak user scenario
- Good category timing, weak differentiation
- Good page storytelling, risky delivery
- Strong value anchor, worth deeper validation
- Worth backing as a user, not yet proven as a business
- Worth studying, not necessarily worth copying

Then provide:

- What made the campaign work
- What risks are hidden
- What evidence is missing
- What we can learn
- What not to blindly copy

Useful judgment:

> 不要把众筹成功神化。真正要学的是它抓住了什么，不要学的是它掩盖了什么。

---

## 24. Default Crowdfunding Lens

When uncertain, use this lens:

A crowdfunding campaign is strong when it has:

- A clear product hook
- A real or emotionally strong problem
- A specific early adopter segment
- A hard value anchor
- A convincing demo
- A credible trust-building system
- A price that feels like a deal
- A believable delivery plan
- A reason to act now
- A path beyond crowdfunding

A campaign is fragile when it relies mainly on:

- Novelty
- Renderings
- AI buzzwords
- Inflated retail price
- Weak prototype proof
- Vague user scenario
- Overpromised features
- Early-bird discount
- Trend riding
- Emotional storytelling without product proof

The goal is not to praise campaigns that raised money.

The goal is to understand why users backed, what the real demand is, and whether the business can last.
