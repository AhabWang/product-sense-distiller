# Jobs Lens

This file defines the Jobs Lens for Product Sense Distiller.

This lens does not imitate Steve Jobs' voice.

It extracts a product thinking perspective inspired by Jobs-style product judgment:

- Simplicity
- End-to-end experience
- Hardware-software integration
- Emotional clarity
- Focus
- Taste
- Product as a complete experience, not a feature bundle

Use this lens only as an auxiliary perspective.

The final judgment must return to Wang Product Philosophy.

---

## 1. Core Question

The Jobs Lens asks:

> Is this product simple, coherent, emotionally clear, and complete enough for users to understand, desire, and use without thinking too much?

This lens does not start from feature count.

It starts from:

- What is the product really about?
- What should users feel?
- What should disappear from the user's burden?
- What complexity should the product absorb internally?
- What should be cut?
- What is the one clear experience this product must own?

Useful judgment:

> 好产品不是把复杂性丢给用户，而是把复杂性藏在系统背后，把简单性留给用户。

---

## 2. When to Use This Lens

Use Jobs Lens when evaluating:

- Consumer hardware
- Smart devices
- AI hardware
- Wearables
- Home products
- Baby products
- Sleep and emotional products
- Premium products
- Products with too many features
- Products with unclear first-use experience
- Products that need stronger emotional resonance
- Products that require hardware-software-content-service integration

Especially use it when the question is:

- Is this product too complicated?
- Is the product experience coherent?
- Does the product have a clear soul?
- Should we cut features?
- Is the product premium enough?
- Does the user understand it immediately?
- Does the whole system feel integrated?

Do not use it as a generic praise tool.

Use it to cut through clutter.

---

## 3. Core Product Beliefs

### 3.1 Simplicity is not fewer features. It is clearer priority.

Simplicity does not mean the product has no depth.

It means users do not need to understand the internal complexity.

Ask:

- What is the one thing this product must do beautifully?
- What can be hidden?
- What can be automated?
- What can be removed?
- What can be delayed to later versions?
- What is making the user think too much?

Useful judgment:

> 简单不是功能少，而是优先级清楚。

---

### 3.2 Product is the whole experience, not the device

A product is not only hardware.

It includes:

- First impression
- Packaging
- Setup
- Physical interaction
- Software flow
- Feedback
- Error recovery
- Daily use
- Maintenance
- Emotional memory
- Brand expectation
- After-sales

Ask:

- Does the experience feel designed as one whole?
- Where does the experience break?
- Which part feels like another team made it?
- What happens before and after the core function?
- Does the product feel complete?

Useful judgment:

> 用户买到的不是一个功能点，而是一整段体验。

---

### 3.3 Focus creates power

A product that tries to do everything usually owns nothing.

Ask:

- What is the product's main promise?
- What should the product be famous for?
- What is the killer experience?
- Which feature dilutes the story?
- Which feature creates internal complexity but no user love?
- Which feature should be cut even if the team likes it?

Useful judgment:

> 产品没有焦点，就没有记忆点；没有记忆点，就很难形成购买理由。

---

### 3.4 Taste is a product capability

Taste is not decoration.

Taste means making the right trade-offs in:

- Form
- Material
- Interaction
- Motion
- Sound
- Visual hierarchy
- Copywriting
- Error states
- Setup flow
- Physical comfort
- Packaging
- Brand feeling

Ask:

- Does this product feel considered?
- Does it feel cheap where it should feel trustworthy?
- Does it look over-designed?
- Does it communicate the right category?
- Does it create trust before function is explained?

Useful judgment:

> 审美不是表面功夫，审美是产品判断力在细节上的体现。

---

### 3.5 Technology should become magic, not homework

Users should not need to understand why the technology is hard.

They should feel the result.

Ask:

- What user-visible improvement does the technology create?
- Can users feel it immediately?
- Does the technology reduce effort?
- Does it create delight?
- Does it create trust?
- Or does it only make the spec sheet look better?

Useful judgment:

> 用户不是为“你做得很难”付钱，而是为“我用起来更简单、更好、更舒服”付钱。

---

## 4. Key Evaluation Questions

When using Jobs Lens, ask:

### Product Essence

- What is this product really about?
- If we remove all marketing language, what remains?
- What is the one sentence product truth?
- What should users remember after seeing it once?

### Simplicity

- Is the core value obvious?
- Can users understand it in 10 seconds?
- Does first use require too much explanation?
- Are there too many modes, buttons, flows, or claims?
- What can be removed?

### End-to-end Experience

- Does the experience feel coherent from unboxing to daily use?
- Is setup simple?
- Is the first successful moment clear?
- What happens when the product fails?
- Does the app support or interrupt the experience?
- Does the physical product and software feel like one system?

### Emotional Pull

- Does the product create desire?
- Does it feel cold, useful, warm, premium, playful, trustworthy, or medical?
- Is that feeling aligned with the category?
- Does the product have a reason to be loved, not just used?

### Focus and Trade-off

- What is the hero experience?
- What is the product overdoing?
- What is the product under-delivering?
- What should be cut?
- What should be made more perfect?

Useful judgment:

> Jobs Lens 不问“还能加什么”，先问“什么必须砍掉，才能让核心体验更强”。

---

## 5. Red Flags

Jobs Lens should be skeptical when seeing:

- Too many features
- Too many modes
- Too many user segments
- Too many claims
- Setup takes too long
- App is required for basic use
- Product story is hard to explain
- Hardware and software feel disconnected
- Form factor does not match scenario
- Premium pricing without premium experience
- Technology is visible but value is unclear
- Users need education before they can understand the product
- Product looks impressive but feels emotionally empty
- Product tries to be everything and becomes nothing

Useful judgment:

> 如果用户要先理解一堆概念，才能知道这个产品为什么好，说明产品本身还不够清楚。

---

## 6. Jobs Lens Output Structure

When the user asks to apply Jobs Lens, use this structure:

1. Jobs Lens verdict
2. The product's real essence
3. What feels coherent
4. What feels unnecessarily complex
5. End-to-end experience gaps
6. Emotional resonance
7. What to cut
8. What to make more perfect
9. Return to Wang Product Philosophy for final judgment

Do not write a long biography.

Do not imitate Jobs.

Do not overuse words like “insanely great.”

Keep the lens product-focused.

---

## 7. Example Application: Bedtime Companion

When applying Jobs Lens to Bedtime Companion:

### Core question

Is Bedtime Companion a simple embodied sleep companion, or is it becoming a feature-heavy AI toy?

### What to protect

- Soft body
- Hug interaction
- Breathing rhythm
- Warmth
- Low-stimulation bedtime experience
- Gentle companionship

### What to be careful about

- Too much AI conversation
- Too many modes
- Too much app control
- Screen interaction before sleep
- Overexplaining the science
- Medical insomnia claims

### Jobs Lens judgment

Bedtime Companion's core should be extremely simple:

> Hold it, breathe with it, feel accompanied, calm down.

If the product requires users to configure too much, chat too much, or think too much, it will violate the bedtime scenario.

Useful judgment:

> Bedtime Companion 的 Jobs Lens 重点不是“加更多 AI”，而是把睡前安抚体验做得足够简单、完整、自然。

---

## 8. Example Application: Bottle Washer

When applying Jobs Lens to a baby bottle washer:

### Core question

Does the product make bottle cleaning feel obviously easier, or does it create another machine parents need to manage?

### What to protect

- Simple loading
- Clear cycle
- Visible clean result
- Dry and ready output
- Easy maintenance
- Clear status feedback
- Low cognitive burden

### What to be careful about

- Too many modes
- App dependency
- Complex maintenance
- Large countertop footprint
- Hard-to-clean internal structure
- Premium price without premium simplicity

### Jobs Lens judgment

The product should make parents feel:

> I put dirty bottles in. I get clean, dry, ready bottles out. I do not need to think too much.

Useful judgment:

> 奶瓶清洗机的高级感，不是 App 功能多，而是让疲惫的父母少操一份心。

---

## 9. How to Combine with Wang Product Philosophy

Jobs Lens improves clarity and focus.

But final judgment must return to Wang Product Philosophy.

After using Jobs Lens, still ask:

- Is the demand strong?
- Is the purchase trigger clear?
- Is the value anchor hard enough?
- Does the price anchor make sense?
- Can hardware execution support the experience?
- Can the business work?
- What evidence is missing?

Useful judgment:

> Jobs Lens 可以帮我们把产品做得更简单、更完整；王师傅主脑要继续判断它值不值得做、能不能卖、能不能长期成立。

---

## 10. Final Jobs Lens Rule

When uncertain, use this rule:

If the product cannot be understood quickly, used simply, and remembered clearly, it is not focused enough.

Cut until the core experience becomes obvious.

Then check whether that core experience is strong enough to support purchase.
