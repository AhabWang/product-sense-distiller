# Marty Cagan Lens

This file defines the Marty Cagan Lens for Product Sense Distiller.

This lens does not imitate Marty Cagan's voice.

It extracts a product thinking perspective inspired by Marty Cagan-style product judgment:

- Product Discovery
- Evidence-based product decisions
- Four product risks
- Empowered product teams
- Outcome over output
- Continuous discovery
- Risk reduction before delivery

Use this lens only as an auxiliary perspective.

The final judgment must return to Wang Product Philosophy.

---

## 1. Core Question

The Marty Cagan Lens asks:

> Before we build this product, have we validated the most important risks?

This lens does not start with:

- What features should we build?
- How fast can we ship?
- What does the roadmap say?
- What does the stakeholder want?

It starts with:

- Do users want this?
- Can users use this?
- Can we build this?
- Can the business support this?
- What evidence do we have?
- What assumption is still unproven?
- What risk should be tested before delivery?

Useful judgment:

> 好产品不是靠写需求写出来的，而是靠不断发现、验证和降低风险推出来的。

---

## 2. When to Use This Lens

Use Marty Cagan Lens when evaluating:

- Product discovery
- New product concepts
- PRD readiness
- Charter readiness
- MVP planning
- Prototype validation
- Hardware development gates
- Go / No-Go decisions
- Feature prioritization
- User research plans
- PMF validation
- Product team process

Especially use it when the question is:

- Should we build this?
- What should we validate first?
- Is the evidence strong enough?
- Are we jumping into delivery too early?
- What is the riskiest assumption?
- Is this output-driven or outcome-driven?
- Is the team empowered to solve the problem?

---

## 3. The Four Product Risks

Every product idea must pass four risks.

### 3.1 Value Risk

Will users buy it or choose to use it?

Ask:

- Do users really have this problem?
- Is the problem important enough?
- Do users understand the value?
- Are users willing to pay?
- Is the value stronger than existing alternatives?
- Is there a clear purchase trigger?
- Does the product create repeat use?

Useful judgment:

> Value Risk 是第一风险。用户不要，其他都白搭。

---

### 3.2 Usability Risk

Can users figure out how to use it?

Ask:

- Can users understand the product?
- Can they complete the key task?
- Is the first-use flow clear?
- Does the interaction match user expectations?
- Does the product reduce or increase cognitive load?
- Can users recover from mistakes?
- Is the product easy enough for the real scenario?

Useful judgment:

> 用户想要，不代表用户会用；用户会用，不代表用户愿意一直用。

---

### 3.3 Feasibility Risk

Can we build it with available technology, time, cost, and capability?

Ask:

- Can the team build this?
- Is the technology mature?
- Are key components available?
- Can the hardware meet requirements?
- Can the app, firmware, and cloud work together?
- Can the algorithm deliver reliable output?
- Can the product pass certification?
- Can this be manufactured consistently?

Useful judgment:

> Feasibility 不是“理论上能做”，而是“在预算、周期、团队和供应链条件下能稳定做出来”。

---

### 3.4 Business Viability Risk

Can this work for the business?

Ask:

- Does the price support margin?
- Does BOM support gross margin?
- Can the channel sell it?
- Can CAC be justified?
- Can after-sales cost be controlled?
- Does it fit brand strategy?
- Does it fit company capability?
- Does it create long-term value?
- Does it strengthen the organization?

Useful judgment:

> 商业可行性不是“有人买”，而是买完之后公司还能健康地活下去。

---

## 4. Discovery Before Delivery

Do not rush into building before discovery.

Delivery answers:

> Can we ship the product?

Discovery answers:

> Should we build this product, and what should it be?

Ask:

- What problem are we solving?
- What evidence proves the problem matters?
- What solution options exist?
- Which option is most promising?
- Which assumptions are unvalidated?
- What is the cheapest way to test them?
- What should be learned before development starts?
- What should be learned before tooling?
- What should be learned before launch?

Useful judgment:

> 不要把开发当验证。开发是最贵的验证方式之一。

---

## 5. Outcome Over Output

Do not measure product work by how many features are delivered.

Measure whether the product creates the intended outcome.

Ask:

- What outcome are we trying to create?
- What user behavior should change?
- What business result should improve?
- What evidence shows progress?
- Does this feature directly support the outcome?
- If we ship this feature and nothing changes, what does that mean?

Bad output goals:

- Ship AI conversation
- Add app control
- Add five modes
- Launch version 2.0
- Complete PRD

Better outcome goals:

- Increase first-week repeat use
- Reduce manual cleaning steps
- Improve first-use success rate
- Increase purchase conversion
- Reduce return rate
- Improve user trust
- Improve 7-day retention

Useful judgment:

> 功能上线不是结果，用户行为改变才是结果。

---

## 6. Evidence Quality

Not all evidence is equal.

Classify evidence strength.

### Weak Evidence

- Users say they like it
- Survey preference
- Social media likes
- Internal opinions
- Expert guesses
- Competitive feature matching

### Medium Evidence

- Users join waitlist
- Users request demo
- Users spend time testing prototype
- Users compare alternatives
- Users ask price
- Users share with others

### Strong Evidence

- Users pay
- Users pre-order
- Users use repeatedly
- Users recommend
- Users complain when removed
- Users continue after novelty fades
- Users accept target price

Ask:

- What evidence do we have?
- What behavior does the evidence represent?
- Is it strong enough for the next decision?
- Are we mistaking weak evidence for strong evidence?

Useful judgment:

> 用户说喜欢是弱证据，用户持续使用和付钱才是强证据。

---

## 7. Prototype Strategy

Prototype to learn, not to impress.

Different risks need different prototypes.

### For Value Risk

Use:

- Concept card
- Landing page
- Ad test
- Interview
- Pre-order test
- Fake-door test
- Crowdfunding pre-launch

### For Usability Risk

Use:

- Clickable prototype
- Foam model
- Wizard-of-Oz flow
- First-use test
- Task completion test
- In-home test

### For Feasibility Risk

Use:

- Technical prototype
- Component test
- Engineering mockup
- Sensor validation
- Algorithm validation
- EVT prototype

### For Business Viability Risk

Use:

- Pricing test
- BOM model
- CAC test
- Channel feedback
- Gross margin model
- Service cost estimate
- Retail buyer feedback

Useful judgment:

> 原型不是为了看起来像产品，而是为了回答一个关键问题。

---

## 8. Continuous Discovery

Discovery is not a one-time phase.

It should continue throughout product development.

Ask:

- Are we continuously talking to target users?
- Are we observing real behavior?
- Are we learning from support and reviews?
- Are we updating assumptions?
- Are we testing alternatives?
- Are we validating changes before scaling?
- Are insights entering product decisions?

Useful judgment:

> Discovery 不是项目前期做一轮访谈就结束，而是团队持续校准现实的机制。

---

## 9. Empowered Team

A product team should not only receive requirements.

It should own problems and outcomes.

Ask:

- Is the team given a problem or a solution?
- Does the team understand the user?
- Does the team understand the business constraint?
- Can the team propose alternatives?
- Can the team say no to weak ideas?
- Can product, design, engineering, research, marketing, and supply chain work as one system?
- Is the team optimizing output or outcome?

Useful judgment:

> 被动接需求的团队，只能交付功能；真正被授权的团队，才可能交付结果。

---

## 10. Product Discovery for Hardware

For hardware, discovery must happen before expensive commitments.

Especially before:

- Tooling
- Certification investment
- Large component orders
- Mass production
- Crowdfunding launch
- Retail launch

Ask:

- Is the user problem validated?
- Is the core experience validated?
- Is the hardware form validated?
- Is comfort validated?
- Is maintenance burden validated?
- Is price validated?
- Is BOM validated?
- Is feasibility validated?
- Is certification path validated?
- Is after-sales risk understood?

Useful judgment:

> 硬件的 discovery 做晚了，后面每一次改错都很贵。

---

## 11. Gate Decision Questions

Use Marty Cagan Lens at decision gates.

### Before Charter Approval

Ask:

- Is the opportunity real?
- Is the target user clear?
- Is the problem worth solving?
- Are key assumptions identified?
- Is the first validation plan clear?

### Before PRD Lock

Ask:

- Is the value proposition validated?
- Is the product boundary clear?
- Are must-have and must-not-do defined?
- Is the first-use KXI clear?
- Are technical risks understood?

### Before EVT

Ask:

- Is the solution direction worth engineering investment?
- Are the hardest technical questions identified?
- Is the prototype plan tied to risk validation?

### Before DVT

Ask:

- Is design direction validated?
- Are user experience requirements stable?
- Are reliability and certification risks being closed?

### Before PVT

Ask:

- Is manufacturing process validated?
- Is yield acceptable?
- Are quality standards clear?
- Is after-sales plan ready?

### Before Launch

Ask:

- Is value proven?
- Is delivery ready?
- Is support ready?
- Is marketing aligned with real capability?
- Is the business case still valid?

Useful judgment:

> 每个 gate 都不是过流程，而是决定下一笔钱、下一段时间、下一轮风险值不值得投入。

---

## 12. Red Flags

Marty Cagan Lens should be skeptical when seeing:

- Product starts from solution, not problem
- PRD written before discovery
- Stakeholder opinions treated as user evidence
- Features prioritized without outcome
- No clear value risk validation
- No prototype before development
- No target user clarity
- No must-not-do list
- No pricing evidence
- No business viability thinking
- No usability testing
- No technical risk spike
- Hardware enters tooling before core assumptions are proven
- Team cannot say no
- Roadmap is output-based only
- Success metric is launch date, not user or business outcome

Useful judgment:

> 如果一个团队只是在按计划交付功能，而不是持续降低风险，这不是产品发现，是项目管理。

---

## 13. Marty Cagan Lens Output Structure

When the user asks to apply Marty Cagan Lens, use this structure:

1. Marty Cagan Lens verdict
2. Key assumptions
3. Value Risk
4. Usability Risk
5. Feasibility Risk
6. Business Viability Risk
7. Evidence quality
8. Discovery gaps
9. Next validation plan
10. Return to Wang Product Philosophy for final judgment

Do not imitate Marty Cagan's voice.

Do not turn the answer into abstract theory.

Make the risks and next validation concrete.

---

## 14. Example Application: Bedtime Companion

When applying Marty Cagan Lens to Bedtime Companion:

### Core question

Before building full hardware, what must be proven?

### Key assumptions

- Users have repeated bedtime anxiety, loneliness, or high arousal.
- Users are willing to use an embodied object before sleep.
- Hug, breathing, warmth, or pressure can create perceived calming.
- AI conversation supports calming rather than stimulation.
- Users will repeat use after novelty fades.
- Users are willing to pay enough to support hardware cost.

### Value Risk

Need to validate:

- Is the bedtime need strong enough?
- Which value proposition converts better: AI companionship, embodied calming, sleep transition, emotional comfort?
- Are users willing to pay?

### Usability Risk

Need to validate:

- Can users use it naturally in bed?
- Does the product reduce or increase bedtime friction?
- Does conversation know when to stop?
- Is the app too intrusive?

### Feasibility Risk

Need to validate:

- Soft body
- Breathing mechanism
- Warmth safety
- Battery
- Fabric cleaning
- AI latency and privacy
- Durability

### Business Viability Risk

Need to validate:

- BOM
- Price acceptance
- CAC
- Return risk
- Safety liability
- Long-term retention

### Marty Cagan Lens judgment

Bedtime Companion should not jump directly to full-function hardware.

The next step should isolate the riskiest assumptions:

- Does embodied calming work?
- Does AI help or hurt?
- Will users repeat use?
- Will users pay?

Useful judgment:

> 睡前陪伴硬件的下一步不是做完整机器人，而是用最小证据验证“具身安抚是否真的改变睡前行为”。

## 15. Example Application: Bottle Washer

When applying Marty Cagan Lens to a bottle washer:

### Core question

Before committing to product definition, what risks must be reduced?

### Value Risk

Need to validate:

- How painful is bottle cleaning?
- Which users feel it most strongly?
- Is washing + drying + hygiene confidence a must-have?
- What price is acceptable?
- What feature actually drives purchase?

### Usability Risk

Need to validate:

- Can users load bottles correctly?
- Is operation simple enough for tired caregivers?
- Is maintenance acceptable?
- Is status feedback clear?

### Feasibility Risk

Need to validate:

- Cleaning performance
- Drying performance
- Bottle compatibility
- Water system
- Noise
- Reliability
- Food-contact safety

### Business Viability Risk

Need to validate:

- BOM
- Gross margin
- Channel price
- Return rate
- Customer support burden
- Reviews and trust

### Marty Cagan Lens judgment

The product should not be evaluated only by feature completeness.

It should be evaluated by whether it reduces caregiver burden enough to justify cost and complexity.

Useful judgment:

> 奶瓶清洗机的 discovery 重点不是用户要不要“智能”，而是用户愿不愿意为“少洗、放心、可持续”付钱。

---

## 16. Example Application: Crowdfunding

When applying Marty Cagan Lens to crowdfunding:

### Core question

What did the campaign actually validate?

A campaign may validate:

- Hook
- Early adopter interest
- Price under discount
- Story conversion
- Waitlist quality
- Prototype trust

It may not validate:

- Retail price
- Long-term retention
- Manufacturing reliability
- After-sales cost
- Mainstream demand
- Channel scalability

Ask:

- Which risk did crowdfunding reduce?
- Which risk remains?
- What evidence is strong?
- What evidence is weak?
- What must be tested after funding?

Useful judgment:

> 众筹不是 PMF，它只是 discovery 的一部分。筹到钱之后，真正的产品风险还在后面。

---

## 17. How to Combine with Wang Product Philosophy

Marty Cagan Lens helps structure product discovery and risk validation.

But final judgment must return to Wang Product Philosophy.

After using this lens, still ask:

- Is the opportunity real?
- Is this our opportunity?
- Is the target user precise?
- Is the demand strong?
- Is the value anchor hard enough?
- Does the price anchor make sense?
- Can hardware reality support the promise?
- Can the business and organization benefit?

Useful judgment:

> Marty Cagan Lens 可以帮我们把风险前置；王师傅主脑要继续判断这个机会到底值不值得下注。

---

## 18. Final Marty Cagan Lens Rule

When uncertain, use this rule:

Do not ask what to build first.

Ask what must be true first.

Then design the smallest valid test to learn whether it is true.
